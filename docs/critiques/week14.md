# Week 14 Self-Critique

**Scope of this critique.** Week 14 retired the Gemma 3 1B IT pipeline and ran a single configuration: `notebooks/week14_wordle_es_lora_run.ipynb` with `RUN_PROFILE="qwen_full"` ŌĆö `Qwen/Qwen3-1.7B` base, LoRA r=8 on `q/k/v/o_proj`, `ACTION_GRANULARITY="char"` (autoregressive 5-letter generation under a vocabulary trie mask), supervised warm-start, then ES on a `VOCAB_SCHEDULE=[16, 32, 64]` curriculum at `N_POP=32`, `N_ITERATIONS=100/stage`. The smoke profile (DistilGPT-2, two ES iters) is plumbing-only and not a research baseline. The critique below is about why both the architecture swap (Gemma ŌåÆ Qwen3) and the action-space swap (single-softmax word head ŌåÆ trie-masked char generation) failed to recover the Week-12-Session-8 result of "ES adds real signal at vocab = 16," and what that means for whether the project should keep training a generic LM from scratch on Wordle at all.

## ORIENT

### Strengths

- **The architecture swap was a clean experiment.** Holding warm-start, ES, fitness shaping, and CRN constant while swapping the base LM (`google/gemma-3-1b-it` ŌåÆ `Qwen/Qwen3-1.7B`) and the action representation (single-softmax head ŌåÆ trie-masked char generation) lets us read the result as a statement about the design space, not about a particular hyperparameter. We did not get a positive answer, but we got an interpretable one.
- **`ACTION_GRANULARITY="char"` is wired end-to-end and runnable on `qwen_full`.** The `_rollout_batched` path in `src/es_wordle.py` already routed through `sample_words_batch` when `action_granularity="char"`, so the new generation path slotted in without a refactor of the optimizer. The smoke profile on DistilGPT-2 caught two prompt-truncation bugs before the long Qwen3 run.
- **The `fb%` (trie fallback rate) diagnostic was load-bearing.** Adding `fb%` and `trie_steps` to the verbose-log row (one extra `trie_stats()` hook on the policy class, ~15 LoC in `_rollout_batched`) is what identified the failure mode ŌĆö without it the run looks like another flat `dprobe`/`cos(─Ø)` story and we are guessing whether the issue is the LM, the action space, or the optimizer. Persistent 60-90% `fb%` says "the trie mask is doing the structural work, the LM is contributing close-to-uniform-over-legal," which is a falsifiable mechanistic claim.
- **Run/checkpoint isolation by profile is preserved.** The Week-12 convention of saving to `models/wordle_qwen_es_head.<RUN_PROFILE>.pt` carried over from `wordle_gemma_*` naming, so the Qwen smoke run cannot clobber the long Qwen production run.
- **Cheapest-experiment-first was honored on the new diagnostic side, not on the model side.** Adding `fb%` was a one-line policy hook + a column in the verbose log. That was the right thing to do first. (The same rule applied to model selection would have meant evaluating Qwen3 zero-shot before training ŌĆö see "wrong-layer debugging" in *Areas for Improvement*.)

### Areas for Improvement

- **The headline outcome is FAIL on the pre-registered rubric and we should say so plainly.** Stage 1 (`VOCAB_SCHEDULE=[16]`) at `N_ITERATIONS=100`: post-WS `Success` = 28% greedy, `best_greedy = 34%` (`+6pp`, inside single-slate noise), `final_greedy = 22%` (`ŌłÆ6pp`, inside single-slate noise), `dprobe` non-zero on 9/100 iters (vs 25% pass floor). Stage 2 (`VOCAB_SCHEDULE=[32]`) was worse. The Week-12 Session-8 PASS-A pattern (`+22pp` best, `+16pp` final, 58% `dprobe` non-zero) did **not** reproduce on Qwen3 + char-mode at vocab = 16. We do not have evidence that Qwen3 + char-mode + ES + LoRA optimizes at all in this configuration.
- **Char-mode under a trie mask is doing nothing useful for ES at SNR.** The mechanism implied by the 60-90% `fb%` is that the LM and the trie mask are operating at cross-purposes: the LM's pre-mask distribution puts most of its probability on letters that do not extend any legal Wordle prefix (because the LM was not trained for this constrained-emission task), so the mask redirects almost every token to the top legal alternative. The post-mask distribution the policy actually samples from is therefore approximately uniform over each prefix's legal continuations ŌĆö and a uniform-over-legal sampling distribution has almost no LoRA-controlled gradient surface for ES to push against. We did not predict this in the Week 14 plan; we should have.
- **Two confounded changes at once.** Week 14 swapped the base LM (Gemma 3 1B IT ŌåÆ Qwen3 1.7B) **and** the action representation (word-softmax ŌåÆ char-mask) in the same experiment. The FAIL is therefore unattributable: we cannot say whether Qwen3 with the *original* word-softmax head would have worked, nor whether Gemma with the *new* char-mode would have worked. Both of those are now followups we owe the record. (Practical reading: given the Week 16 plan to skip our own training entirely, the value of running them is low ŌĆö they would only refine the post-mortem.)
- **The Qwen3 + char-mode attempt cost a full week of compute on the same wrong-layer pattern Week 12 critiqued.** Week 12's Session-8 critique introduced the rule "when a metric is flat across multiple model/estimator variants, the next hypothesis should be a property of the data the estimator sees, not another variant of the model." Week 14 is a textbook violation: we changed the LM and the action head together, but we did not change the per-secret revisit count, the eval slate composition, or the warm-start design ŌĆö even though the Session-8 result said the per-secret revisit count was the binding constraint at vocab = 16 *before* Week 14 ran. The Week 14 ES configuration uses `n_eval_episodes=32` against a 16-secret stage-1 pool (2├Ś visits per secret, below the 4├Ś regime where Test B and Session 8 both found signal). Either we should have raised `n_eval_episodes` to 64 first or we should have re-enabled `per_iter_secret_subset_size` from Session 8's plumbing ŌĆö neither was done.
- **The pre-registered pass criterion was a Week-12 carryover and was probably wrong for char-mode.** "`best_greedy ŌłÆ post_ws_greedy Ōēź +10pp` AND `final_greedy Ōēź post_ws_greedy` AND `dprobe` non-zero on Ōēź25% of iters" was tuned for the word-softmax head, where `dprobe` measures whether the perturbed policy's argmax over a 2,315-way classifier moves on the held-out probe. In char-mode, `dprobe` measures whether the masked autoregressive sampler produces a different *5-character word* on the held-out probe ŌĆö a strictly higher-bar event, especially when `fb%` is 60-90% and the mask is dominating. We should have either lowered the `dprobe` threshold for char-mode or replaced it with a per-letter argmax-shift count.
- **No ablation of the warm-start.** Week 12 already showed warm-start does the bulk of the lifting and ES adds noise on top; Week 14 did not isolate "Qwen3 zero-shot greedy", "Qwen3 + warm-start greedy", "Qwen3 + warm-start + ES greedy" as three separate numbers. The single number we report (post-WS = 28%) collapses two of them. Without the third comparison the FAIL cannot distinguish "Qwen3 char-mode is bad at Wordle" from "warm-start is fine, ES adds nothing."
- **Per-stage runtime made the failure more expensive than it should have been.** Each `qwen_full` curriculum stage at `N_POP=32`, `N_ITERATIONS=100` takes long enough that the failure mode (flat `dprobe`, persistent `fb%`) was visible in the first ~5 iters but not killed until stage 2 had also failed. The smoke profile validated plumbing but not "is the experiment going to teach us anything?" ŌĆö that gap is what the Week-12 `PROBE_VOCAB=4` experiment plugged for the LoRA sweep, and Week 14 has no analogue. A `qwen_probe` profile at `N_POP=8`, `N_ITERATIONS=10`, single stage would have surfaced the failure inside an hour instead of overnight.
- **`n_eval_episodes=32` against a 16-secret pool gives the same lottery-ticket regime Week 12 critiqued.** With 2├Ś visits per secret and `N_POP=32`, the per-population-member fitness is dominated by which two episodes per secret each member happened to play under CRN. Week 12's critique made this point about the production curriculum's `n_eval_episodes=16` against a 16-secret pool (1├Ś per secret); the Week 14 stage-1 design only escapes that regime by a factor of 2 and is well below the 4├Ś threshold Test B / Session 8 found.

### Critical Risks / Assumptions

- **Risk: char-mode-with-trie-mask is the wrong action representation for ES on a generic LM, not a tunable.** The 60-90% `fb%` is structural: it says the trie is the policy and the LM is a noise source. No amount of LoRA, ALPHA scheduling, or `n_eval_episodes` is going to change that. If we run more char-mode experiments without a fundamentally different mask design (e.g. only mask after the LM's own constrained-decoding prior is calibrated to legal-prefix mass), we will reproduce this result.
- **Risk: the project's "warm-start a generic LM, let ES specialize via LoRA on Wordle" thesis is now empirically suspect across two LM families and two action representations.** Weeks 10/12/14 have produced exactly one positive ES result (Week 12 Session 8: `+22pp` at `VOCAB_SCHEDULE=[16]` under quartered ALPHA, mini-batch CRN, Gemma + LoRA + word-softmax). That result has not generalized to (a) larger vocab stages, (b) the full curriculum, (c) Qwen3, or (d) char-mode. The conclusion is not "the thesis is wrong" ŌĆö it is "the thesis is true only inside a narrow operating window we keep failing to leave." Week 16's pivot to Prime Intellect's pre-trained Wordle SFT/RL checkpoints is the right next move precisely because it stops trying to leave that window from inside.
- **Risk (verdict logic): we kept the Week-12 pass criterion verbatim for char-mode.** The criterion is well-calibrated for word-softmax; applying it unchanged to a representation where the binding constraint is `fb%` (not `dprobe`) was a category error. Future critique sessions should re-derive the rubric whenever the action representation changes, not just when the model does.
- **Assumption: Qwen3 1.7B is "stronger" than Gemma 3 1B IT for Wordle.** We assumed this on the basis of qualitative spot-checks of Qwen3's English short-form prior, not on a head-to-head zero-shot Wordle eval. We do not know whether Qwen3 is actually a better starting point for this task; Week 16 will at least pin down the question for the Wordle-specific Qwen3 derivatives.
- **Assumption: the trie mask preserves enough of the LM's distribution to be ES-trainable.** The Week 14 result is direct evidence against this assumption at our SNR. We carried it over from the Week 12 char-mode plan without empirical justification.

## DECIDE

### Concrete Next Actions

1. **Pivot to Prime Intellect's Wordle-specific Qwen checkpoints** (`PrimeIntellect/Qwen3-1.7B-Wordle-SFT` and `ŌĆ”-Wordle-RL`). Replace our own warm-start with their SFT checkpoint, drop char-mode, drop the trie mask, and rollout via `model.generate(...)` parsing `<guess>WORD</guess>`. This is the Week 16 plan; the new policy class is `src/wordle_qwen_policy.py`.
2. **Treat ES as the *only* thing we are training on top of the SFT base.** No re-warm-start, no curriculum staging, no head-only fallback ŌĆö just LoRA r=4 on `q/k/v/o_proj`, the same ES code, and a much narrower question: "does ES add anything on top of an already-Wordle-specialized base, or has the project been training warm-start the whole time?"
3. **Evaluate `ŌĆ”-Wordle-RL` as the reference ceiling.** With a real ceiling number, every future ES report can print `RL_ceiling`, `SFT_cold`, `SFT + ES (final)`, `SFT + ES (best iter)` side by side, and we can finally print an `ES contribution` number that is `final ŌłÆ SFT_cold` instead of `n/a`.
4. **Add a Week-16 probe profile.** A `N_POP=8`, `N_ITERATIONS=10`, single-`MAX_NEW_TOKENS=64` smoke run that exercises generation + parse + ES probe in under an hour. The lack of an equivalent in Week 14 is what made the failure expensive.
5. **Stop running char-mode without a redesign of the mask.** If we ever return to char-mode, the trie-mask hypothesis to test is not "trie mask vs no mask" but "trie mask vs prefix-constrained logit *bias* (additive penalty proportional to how illegal the next character is)" so the LM's pretrained distribution still has gradient against the constraint instead of being overwritten by it.
6. **Backfill the missing single-axis ablations only if they would change the Week 16 plan.** Specifically: Qwen3 + word-softmax (one axis swap from Week 14) and Gemma + char-mode (the other axis swap). If Week 16 succeeds, these become "post-mortem only" and we cite Week 14's FAIL as sufficient. If Week 16 also fails, we owe at least one of these to disambiguate "char-mode is the problem" from "Qwen3 is the problem."

## Postscript: What this critique is actually saying

The strong reading of Weeks 10 / 12 / 14 together is that **for a generic LM warm-started on our small Wordle dataset and trained with ES on LoRA, the productive operating window is narrow enough that we keep falling out of it whenever we change anything**. We exited Week 12 with one positive result (Session 8, `+22pp` at vocab = 16) and a clear next-step plan (mini-batch CRN, normalized gradients, scale to vocab = 32, then re-enable the curriculum). Week 14 did not execute that plan; it executed an orthogonal set of changes (Qwen3 + char-mode) and came back with no signal at the same vocab the Session 8 result was at. This is not a minor revision to the Week 12 picture, it is the project saying it does not know how to make ES scale beyond the Session-8 working point on a generic LM at all. Week 16 acknowledges that and changes the question: instead of asking "can ES train a generic LM to play Wordle," it asks "can ES improve a Wordle-specialized LM somebody else already trained, by any measurable amount." The expected answer of the Week 14 evidence is "no, but we do not yet know whether that is because of ES or because of the warm-start ceiling." Week 16's job is to disambiguate that.
